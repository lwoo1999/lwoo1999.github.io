About Me

I am a lecturer at the School of Mechanical & Electrical Engineering, Chizhou University. I received my B.S. in Astronomy (2020) and Ph.D. in Astrophysics (2025) from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Jun-Xian Wang.

My research leverages multi-wavelength survey data (SDSS, WISE, GALEX) and combines spectroscopic analysis with statistical methods to investigate the physics of active galactic nuclei and quasars. I focus on the interplay and co-evolution between supermassive black holes and their host galaxies.

My work has been published in leading international journals including The Astrophysical Journal Letters (ApJL) and Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society (MNRAS).

Selected Publications

First Author 2026

Tracing Star Formation in Quasar Hosts via [O II] λ3727: A Kinematically Consistent Approach

Liang Wu, Jun-Xian Wang, Luis C. Ho, Junfeng Wang, Zhicheng He

The Astrophysical Journal Letters, 1001, L15

DOI · arXiv · ADS
Abstract

We develop a new kinematically consistent approach that decomposes the [O II] λ3727 emission line using the high-ionization [Ne V] λ3426 line to isolate the AGN contribution. We demonstrate that [O II] emission in quasars is primarily dominated by star formation and can be reliably used as a star formation tracer. Applying this method to a large SDSS quasar sample, we uncover a tight correlation between the mean star formation rate and bolometric luminosity, revealing continuous star formation activity across cosmic time in quasar hosts.

First Author 2024

Is Quasar Variability Regulated by the Close Environment of Accretion?

Liang Wu, Jun-Xian Wang, Wen-Ke Ren, Wen-Yong Kang

Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, 533(1), 908–917

DOI · arXiv · ADS
Abstract

We investigate whether UV/optical variability in quasars is influenced by the close environment of the accretion flow. Analyzing two luminous quasar samples from SDSS (8,120 sources), we find a significant anti-correlation between g-band excess variance and the wavelength-dependent infrared covering factor, strongest at 2–3 μm and weakening toward longer wavelengths. This provides evidence that the equatorial dusty torus plays a key role in regulating accretion disk turbulence, while cooler polar dust does not.

First Author 2023

Ensemble Mapping the Inner Structure of Luminous Quasars

Liang Wu, Jun-Xian Wang, Hao-Chen Wang, Wen-Yong Kang, Wei-Da Hu, Ting-Gui Wang, Hui-Yuan Wang

Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society, 522(1), 1108–1117

DOI · arXiv · ADS
Abstract

We present an ensemble mapping technique to probe the inner structure of luminous quasars. Using correlations between [O III] λ5007 equivalent width and infrared-to-bolometric luminosity ratios, we detect for the first time the intrinsic anti-correlation predicted by the torus/ionization-cone geometric model. Our results provide direct evidence for the polar dust component and verify the orientation-dependent unification scheme in luminous quasars.
